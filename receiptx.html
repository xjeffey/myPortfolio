ReceiptX

Overview

ReceiptX is a mobile application that helps self-motivated people streamline their monthly expenses by identifying and categorizing their financial habits, allowing them to cut back on spending.

This was a capstone project I did for my final year at Indiana University, where I spent most of my spare time working closely with 3 other peers to design and build our application.

Background

When students start their university journey, not only do they grow as a student, they grow as a person. Students also have wants and needs so they learn how to become financially responsible to balance between schooling and personal expenses. However, receipts are easy to lose, making it hard for someone to keep track of their spending.

Understanding the problem

As a university students living on our own, my team and I understood the pain of having to balance between school and personal expenses. We wanted to validate this issue by asking fellow classmates about what they experience through surveys and interviews.

We decided to focus on college students, specifically anyone above first year students (undergraduate) as they transition between living in a dorm to an apartment where rooming feedbacks become monthly instead of a one-time payment.

After listening to my peers, we saw their main issues:

  • No reason to keep receipts. People find it unneccessary to keep receipts because they have no use for it, other than to return an item. This prevents them from tracking their expenses in the future if they need reference the information.
  • Managing receipts is a hassle. Receipts can be stored anywhere convienent, making it hard for people to organize their information. This prevents people from locating a specific receipt if they need to use it.

User personas

Based on our research, we wanted our product to solve the problem for current college students (Persona 1) and postgraduates (Persona 2). We decided to focus on current college students because we had a better chance of contacting the target group since we were on a campus.

Defining the problem statement

After gaining insight from our research, my team and I worked together to define our target group's problem.

Problem statement:

College students living on campus learn how care for themselves but struggle to be financially responsible because they have little visibility over their monthly expenses.

The product vision

For our project, we wanted to focus on 3 key areas:

  • Streamline spending. We want to provide individuals a visualization of their monthly expenses, allowing them to track spending habits.
  • Financial goals. We want to help people become financially responsible by providing them with the ability to set monthly spending goals.
  • Receipt management. We want to give people a single storage location for their receipts, making it easier to find a specific receipt.

Defining the MVP

My team and I went through workshops and sketching sessions to map out the user flow of our application. In addition, we did usability testing with this paper prototype to influence the high fidelity version.

We identified the following key features:

  • Google authentication. Users connect their Google account to link their application data.
  • Manual and image receipt data entry. Receipt data can be entered into our application by taking an image or entering in required information.
  • Review data of a receipt. Display information of receipt to user.
  • Goal setting. Allow users to set monthly spending goals to work towards being financially responsible.
  • Filtering/sorting receipt data Users can select what receipts they want to see on the screen.
  • Graphs of monthly spending View monthly expenses, allowing users to track expenses and indicate spending habits.

Results and Takeaways

Working on ReceiptX was quite an eye-opening experience as it allowed me to put the skills I had developed during my undergraduate journey to the test. The project also taught me a lot abut knowing where to focus my energy and efforts.

Some key take aways from this project are that:

  • Have a plan. These projects take time and effort. Having a plan helps a team estimate where they should be in the project.
  • Plans can alter. Although having a plan is important for any project, they can change accordingly. It is important to be able to adapt to change.
  • Communication is key. Better ideas come with discussion. Talking with teammates also help each other be on the same page during a project.
  • Understand the roles of each team member. No role is too important in a project. Learning about what each team member does allows for better conversations because you can talk in terms they can understand.
