Jeffrey Tran

Rhino Hammer

Overview

This weapon is an ancient “Rhino-Hammer” from W’Kabi’s tribe that has been modified to allow the head to release and extend the length of the weapon. The weapon can also gather and charge kinetic energy by swinging it around and then transferring the stored potential energy in three ways: “Rhino Smash – direct hit release, “Rhino Blast” – concentrated laser-shot release of energy through the horn of the hammer, and “Rhino Quake:” – slamming the rhino horn into the ground releases energy into the surrounding environment.

Role

UX Designer

User Research, Visual Design, Prototyping & Testing

Spring 2018

Background

Black Panther was one of the most culturally significant movies for African Americans in recent history by involving the African culture in a mainstream fashion. Our task was to design anything for a potential Black Panther sequel.

Rhino Hammer helped me establish foundational design skills.

When I was a sophomore during undergraduate, I did not know what UX Design was about. Rhino Hammer allowed to become passionate about the process behind creating a product and helped me grow as a designer. Some of my key achievements listed below:

  • Had a conversation with Senior Producer Eric Daly. It was an eye-opening experience to learn what went behind the scenes of a large scale project like Black Panther. The skills that we are learning will help us in our future careers.
  • Developed interpersonal skills. Sometimes people do not know how to describe their issues. Being able to interact with people to identify their problems is helpful when working on a project.
  • Engaged in team discussions. Being engaged discussions allows a team to come up with better ideas and the project becomes more enjoyable, rather than something they have to do.

Understanding the problem

To learn more about what people are looking for in Black Panther 2, we read online articles, discussion boards, social media posts, examined images, and interviewed local comic book stores. In addition, we also talked to Senior Producer Eric Daly, one of the designers behind Wakanda's technological city.

Additional References

  • Lesothan man dressed in traditional costume
  • Modern African king dressed in traditional clothing and jewelry
  • Traditional African weapons
  • Ancient Zulu warriors
  • Slingshot from the Baule people in the Ivory Coast
  • Black Panther comic scene

After reviewing our research, we saw the following:

  • Cultural authenticity. People loved the authenticity and representation in the film, showing us that must stay culturally sensitive for future designs.
  • Rhinoceroses in the film were exceedingly popular. The rhinos were very popular and marketable as toys.
  • A spear was the main weapon. The main cultural weaponry referenced in the film was the spear. Fans would like to see something different.

The product vision

For our final product, we focused on the following 3 key areas:

  • Culturally rooted. We wanted to continue to represent African culture as people enjoyed what was shown in Black Panther.
  • Other weaponry. Spears were commonly used in the original film. Fans wanted to see something different.
  • Integrate rhinoceros into design. People loved the rhinos, so we wanted to use that knowledge to our advantage by incorporating it into our design.
